Frequently Asked Questions

When should I consult a nephrologist?

You should consult a nephrologist if you experience symptoms like changes in urination, swelling in ankles or face, high blood pressure, or if tests show abnormal kidney function, protein, or blood in the urine.

What are the symptoms of kidney disease?

Early stages often have no symptoms. Advanced symptoms may include fatigue, swelling, difficulty sleeping, muscle cramps, poor appetite, and frequent urination, especially at night.

What dialysis services are available?

We provide comprehensive guidance and management for both hemodialysis and peritoneal dialysis, ensuring you receive the best care suited to your lifestyle and medical needs.

Can kidney disease be prevented?

While not all kidney diseases can be prevented, you can lower your risk by managing blood pressure and diabetes, maintaining a healthy weight, eating a balanced diet, and avoiding excessive use of certain pain medications.
